## 〔 ENGINEERING CHRONICLES 〕
### *The Technical Mind Behind the Design Vision*

</div>

<div align="center">

```
ECE CORE + PRODUCT DESIGN MIND = RARE SIGNAL IN THE NOISE
```

</div>

Most product designers understand the user. Fewer understand the circuit that powers the product. Fewer still understand the mold that gives it its shell, the electrode that burns its cavity, or the embedded system that makes it intelligent.

**I am building all of it simultaneously.**

My Electronics and Communication Engineering foundation gives me something most design students never have: I understand **signal**, **system**, and **hardware** from first principles. When I design a product housing, I am simultaneously thinking about:

- Heat dissipation from the PCB inside
- EMI shielding requirements from the antenna
- Draft angles for the injection mold
- Assembly sequence for the manufacturing line
- The hand that will first open the box

This is not multitasking. This is **integrated thinking** — and it is the exact capability that separates good product designers from extraordinary ones.
